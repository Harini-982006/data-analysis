E-Commerce Sales Performance EDA Report

1. Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the e-commerce sales dataset. The primary objective is to analyze transaction patterns, identify key factors driving revenue, and detect trends across different product categories, cities, months, and payment methods.

Key Milestone Achieved: Outlier correction, duplicate row filters, and imputation methods were executed. The cleaned database represents 51 high-quality transactions totaling ₹1,39,719 in revenue.

Analysis indicates that while Clothing is the leader in transaction counts, Furniture exhibits the highest Average Order Value (AOV) at ₹3,827.27. Mumbai constitutes the primary city hub generating 28.0% of the total sales, and UPI is the favorite transaction option used in 45.1% of orders.

2. Data Preprocessing Summary

To establish accurate analytical findings, the original dataset (63 rows) was processed as follows:

  • Duplicate Filtering: Removed 3 duplicate rows (orders 1005, 1012, 1033) which were redundant transaction repeats.
  • Missing Values: Imputed customer names with "Unknown Customer" (2 rows), categories with "Unknown" (2 rows), and missing quantities with the median value of 1 (3 rows).
  • Extreme Outlier Treatment: Set Price boundaries using IQR method. Extreme anomalies like ₹85,000 for a power strip, negative prices (-₹500), and transactions with zero quantity were removed. 6 high-value transactions with prices above the upper limit (₹6,601) were excluded to focus on the standard consumer distribution.

Post-preprocessing, the final dataset has 51 rows and 13 variables ready for exploration.

4. Correlation & Influencing Factors

Standard Pearson correlation coefficient ($r$) calculations reveal key dynamics:

  • Price vs. Total Sales (r = 0.71): Strong positive linear relationship. Sales values are heavily driven by individual item prices. Higher unit cost products contribute highly to overall revenues.
  • Price vs. Quantity (r = -0.50): Moderate negative correlation. High price products show lower quantity orders. Customers order single units of items like Coffee Tables, while ordering bulk numbers (up to 10) of cheap items like Pen Drives.
  • Quantity vs. Sales (r = 0.04): Very weak/no correlation. Transaction quantity by itself does not predict revenue since bulk orders are concentrated in cheap items.

5. Segment Analysis

A. Performance by Product Category

Clothing leads in total revenue with ₹48,566 (34.8%), followed closely by Electronics at ₹44,955 (32.2%) and Furniture at ₹42,100 (30.1%). Furniture represents the most premium segment with an average transaction value of ₹3,827.27.

B. City-wise Revenue Contribution

Mumbai is the strongest performing city generating ₹39,186 (28.0% of total), followed by Bangalore (₹26,482) and Hyderabad (₹24,891). Chennai (₹12,194) and Kolkata (₹17,979) represent growing markets.

C. Payment Modes and Month Trends

UPI is the leading checkout option (45.1% share), followed by Credit Cards (27.5%). Seasonality check shows sales peaking in March (₹53,180), followed by January (₹38,679) and February (₹33,771).

6. Strategic Actions & Recommendations

1. Optimize Furniture Bundling: Focus on increasing the average order value in the high-ticket Furniture category by bundling tables with chairs or offering special EMI campaigns on checkout.

2. Targeted Metro Ads: Direct promotional spends to Mumbai, Bangalore, and Hyderabad since they constitute over 64% of total sales.

3. UPI-linked Discounts: Offer minor incentives for UPI checkouts to encourage the preferred customer method and save on card processing fees.

4. Replicate March Promos: Investigate March strategies and duplicate similar discount structures in slower periods like February and April.
